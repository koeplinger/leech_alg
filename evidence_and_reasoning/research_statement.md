# Research Statement

## English

In the Leech lattice Λ, the minimal vectors Min(Λ) — the 196,560 vectors
of squared norm 8 — play a structural role analogous to that of the root
system in root lattices.  In the E8 lattice, for example, the 240 roots
close under octonion multiplication (after applying the index-doubling
permutation that corrects Kirmse's integers to Coxeter's integral
octonions), yielding 7 maximal orders, one for each choice of distinguished
imaginary basis element.  Whether an analogous multiplicative structure
exists on Min(Λ) — that is, whether Λ admits an order under some bilinear
product — is not known.

### References

- Conway, J.H. and Smith, D.A., "On Quaternions and Octonions" (2003),
  Chapter 9.  [Maximal orders of integral octonions, index-doubling
  permutation.]
- Coxeter, H.S.M., "Integral Cayley numbers", Duke Math. J. 13 (1946),
  561–578.  [Correction of Kirmse's non-closed integers.]
- Wilson, R.A., "Octonions and the Leech lattice", J. Algebra 322 (2009),
  2186–2190.  [Wilson's characterisation of Λ using three copies of the
  octonion algebra.]

## German (Deutsch)

Im Leech-Gitter Λ spielen die Minimalvektoren Min(Λ) — die 196.560
Vektoren mit Normquadrat 8 — eine strukturelle Rolle, die der des
Wurzelsystems in Wurzelgittern entspricht.  Im E8-Gitter etwa schließen
die 240 Wurzeln unter Oktonionenmultiplikation (nach Anwendung der
Indexverdopplungspermutation, die Kirmses nicht-abgeschlossene ganzen
Zahlen zu Coxeters ganzen Oktonionen korrigiert), und es ergeben sich
7 Maximalordnungen, je eine pro Wahl eines ausgezeichneten imaginären
Basiselements.  Ob eine analoge multiplikative Struktur auf Min(Λ)
existiert — das heißt, ob Λ unter einem bilinearen Produkt eine Ordnung
zulässt — ist nicht bekannt.

## Corrections applied to the user's original draft

1. **"Min(Λ) takes the role of a root system"** → Corrected: Min(Λ) plays
   a structural role *analogous to* a root system.  Technically, a root
   system requires norm-2 vectors and closure under reflections; the Leech
   lattice has no norm-2 vectors.  Min(Λ) consists of norm-4 vectors
   (squared norm 8) that generate Λ and determine its symmetry group, hence
   the analogy.  (Here "squared norm 8" refers to the standard Euclidean
   inner product v · v = Σ vᵢ²; Wilson uses a halved norm under which these
   vectors have norm 4.  This project uses the standard convention
   throughout; see terminology.md, "Leech lattice Λ — norm conventions.")

2. **"Kirmse-twist"** → Clarified as "index-doubling permutation."
   Kirmse (1924) proposed a set of 240 integral octonions forming E8, but
   this set is not closed under multiplication.  Coxeter (1946) showed that
   a specific permutation of the imaginary basis elements corrects this.
   The term "Kirmse twist" is used in this project as shorthand.

3. **"integral orders"** → Corrected to "maximal orders."  A maximal order
   is a lattice in the octonion algebra that is closed under multiplication,
   contains the identity, and is maximal with respect to inclusion.  The 7
   orders obtained via the Kirmse construction are maximal.  "Integral order"
   is less precise.
