Claim number: 007
Title: The triple-octonion algebra (O₁ ⊕ O₂ ⊕ O₃) is comprehensively ruled out as an order on Λ
Status: ESTABLISHED
Date: 2026-04-09

--- STATEMENT ---

The triple-octonion algebra — three copies of the standard octonion algebra
on R²⁴ with Z₃-symmetric cross-block mixing — cannot be an order on the
Leech lattice Λ.  This ruling-out is comprehensive: it applies not only to
the base algebra but to all natural modifications tested (per-block scaling,
conjugation variants, sign flips, routing changes, and E8 automorphism
basis changes).

--- DEFINITION OF THE ALGEBRA FAMILY ---

Base algebra (trial 001):
  O₁ at indices 0–7, O₂ at indices 8–15, O₃ at indices 16–23.
  Same-block:  Oα × Oα → Oα  (standard octonion product).
  Cross-block: Oα × Oβ → Oγ  where {α,β,γ} = {0,1,2}.

Modifications tested:
  (a) Per-block scaling s₁, s₂, s₃ (trial 002)
  (b) Conjugation of left/right factor in cross-block terms:
      4 choices per pair × 3 pairs = 64 combinations (trial 003)
  (c) Sign flips on cross-block contributions:
      2 choices per pair × 3 pairs = 8 combinations (trial 003)
  (d) Routing: third-block, left-block, or right-block (trial 003)
  (e) E8 lattice automorphisms (signed permutations) applied independently
      to each block (trial 004)

Combined discrete space: 64 × 8 × 3 = 1,536 variants (trial 003).
Automorphism search: 500 single-block + 500 three-block random +
128 exhaustive sign-only (trial 004).

--- EVIDENCE ---

Trial 001 — Base algebra (trial_001_triple_octonion.py):
  Result: 74.8% of type3×type3 products fail.
  All other type combinations (t1×t1, t1×t2, t1×t3, t2×t2, t2×t3) pass 100%.
  Failure mode: exclusively Wilson condition 3 (x+y+z ∈ Ls).
  Conditions 1 and 2 are always satisfied.
  Evidence file: evidence_and_reasoning/trial_001_results.md

Trial 002 — Per-block scaling (trial_002_scaled_triple_octonion.py):
  Result: Two independent obstructions.
  Obstruction 1 (norm): For type3×type3, min achievable ||q||² ≈ 14.6 > 8.
    No scaling can bring these products to the minimal shell.
  Obstruction 2 (lattice): Even when ||q||² = 8 is achievable (type2×type2),
    0 of 1,188 products at that norm satisfy Wilson's conditions.
    The scaling introduces irrational coordinate mixing.
  Evidence file: evidence_and_reasoning/trial_002_results.md

Trial 003 — Discrete variants (trial_003_discrete_variants.py):
  Result: All 1,536 variants fail.
  Best closure rate: 68.8% (same as baseline; achieved by 31 variants,
    all with third-block routing).
  Zero variants have zero type3×type3 failures.
  Sign flips have zero effect (all 8 patterns tie when other params fixed).
  Detailed analysis of best variant confirms: failures are exclusively
    type3×type3, exclusively condition 3 (x+y+z ∈ Ls).
  Evidence file: evidence_and_reasoning/trial_003_results.md

Trial 004 — E8 automorphism basis changes (trial_004_basis_automorphisms.py):
  Result: Identity (no change) is already optimal at 60%.
  Single-block automorphisms: best 53.3% (worse than baseline).
  Three-block automorphisms: best 40.0% (much worse).
  Sign-only on one block (exhaustive 128): all tie at 60.0%.
  Automorphisms introduce NEW failure types (type2×type2) not in baseline.
  Evidence file: evidence_and_reasoning/trial_004_results.md

--- STRUCTURAL DIAGNOSIS ---

The failure is intrinsic to the triple-octonion architecture:

1. Type-3 vectors have all three blocks nonzero.  The product generates 9
   nonzero contributions (3 same-block + 6 cross-block) distributed across
   3 target blocks.

2. Wilson's condition 3 requires x+y+z ∈ Ls, where x, y, z are the three
   octonion-block components of the product.  This condition governs the
   *global* relationship between all three blocks simultaneously.

3. The triple-octonion product determines each block's value from a sum of
   three independent octonion products.  The Ls sublattice condition imposes
   a constraint that links all three blocks through a specific algebraic
   relation (multiplication by the structural element s).  The triple-
   octonion architecture has no mechanism to enforce this constraint.

4. This diagnosis is confirmed by the insensitivity to modifications:
   - Conjugation changes the Ls/Ls̄ structure within each cross-block term
     but cannot create the inter-block coordination that condition 3 requires.
   - Sign flips change interference patterns but not the fundamental
     mismatch between the product's block-independence and condition 3's
     block-linking requirement.
   - Routing changes where products land but doesn't change *what* they are.
   - Basis changes within blocks can't create inter-block structure.

--- WHAT IS RULED OUT ---

Any algebra of the form:
  "Three copies of an octonion algebra, with block structure O₁ ⊕ O₂ ⊕ O₃
   mapped to indices {0–7, 8–15, 16–23}, where same-block products use the
   standard octonion product (possibly with conjugation), and cross-block
   products use the octonion product (possibly with conjugation, sign flips,
   or different routing), optionally with per-block scaling or E8 automorphism
   basis changes within each block."

--- WHAT IS NOT RULED OUT ---

1. Algebras where cross-block products use a DIFFERENT product (not the
   octonion product) — e.g., the Okubo product.
2. Algebras with a different block decomposition (not 8+8+8).
3. Products defined directly on R²⁴ without block structure.
4. Products where same-block terms also differ from the standard octonion
   product.
