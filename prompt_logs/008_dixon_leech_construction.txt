Interpreted as: verify that Dixon's 196,560 candidate minimal vectors have
the required properties — correct count, norm 8, rank 24, no duplicates.

Part 2: "can it be interpreted as a 24-dimensional integral algebra that is
a module over the integers that closes on the minimal-vector shell."

structure?  No idea yet."  The computational tools built here (leech_dixon.py
and test_leech_dixon.py) provide the groundwork for future investigation.

--- CONSTRUCTION INVESTIGATED ---

Dixon's Leech lattice [Dixon2010 Section 6]:

Three families of 196,560 minimal vectors, where:
  A^odd  = A₁ ∪ A₃ (240 norm-2 elements; Dixon's rational E8 representation)
  A^even = Ξ^even  (240 norm-1 elements)
  ℓ₀ = ½(e₀+e₁+...+e₇)  (Dixon's base element, N(ℓ₀) = 2)

  Type 1 [eq. 22]:  (2P, 0, 0) + cyclic perms,  P ∈ A^odd
  Type 2 [eq. 21]:  (2Q, ±2e_aQ, 0) + cyclic perms,  Q ∈ A^even, a ∈ {0..7}
  Type 3 [eq. 20]:  (P, ±e_aP, ±(e_aℓ₀)(e_cP)) + cyclic perms,
                    P ∈ A^odd, a,c ∈ {0..7}, two independent ± signs

  Count: 240×3 + 240×8×2×3 + 240×8×8×2×2×3 = 720 + 11,520 + 184,320 = 196,560.

--- CRITICAL FINDING: A^ODD vs WILSON'S E8 ROOTS ---

Dixon's A^odd = A₁ ∪ A₃ where A₃ has an EVEN number of minus signs.
Wilson's E8 roots = A₁ ∪ {half-integer elements with an ODD # of minus signs}.

These two 128-element half-integer families are DISJOINT — they partition all
2^8 = 256 sign patterns, with A₃ taking even and Wilson taking odd.

Consequence for type-1: for P ∈ A₃, the vector (2P, 0, 0) requires 2P ∈ Ls̄
(Wilson's condition 2).  But 2P has integer coordinates ±1 that are not all
even, so 2P ∉ 2L = Ls̄ ∩ Ls.  Therefore all 384 type-1 vectors with P ∈ A₃
FAIL Wilson's is_in_leech.  This is verified computationally.

--- CRITICAL FINDING: DIFFERENT EMBEDDING ---

Dixon's construction gives a DIFFERENT EMBEDDING of the Leech lattice from
Wilson's.  The two 196,560-vector shells overlap in exactly 17,232 vectors:

  Type-1 overlap:  336  (the 112 A₁ elements × 3 cyclic perms; A₁ is common
                   to both constructions.  The remaining 384 Dixon type-1
                   vectors are NOT in Wilson's shell.)
  Type-2 overlap:  6,144  (of 11,520 Dixon type-2 vectors)
  Type-3 overlap:  10,752  (of 184,320 Dixon type-3 vectors)
  Total shared:    17,232  out of 196,560

  Dixon-only:  179,328 vectors not in Wilson's Λ.
  Wilson-only: 179,328 vectors not in Dixon's shell.

Both constructions produce 196,560 distinct vectors of norm 8 that span R^24.
Dixon's shell and Wilson's shell are different embeddings of the Leech lattice
minimal-vector shell in the ambient R^24 = L³.

--- KEY FINDINGS ---

1. Dixon's construction produces 196,560 DISTINCT vectors of norm 8 spanning
   R^24 — the basic structural requirements for a Leech lattice minimal shell.

2. Dixon's type-1 uses A^odd = A₁ ∪ A₃ (EVEN # of minus signs), while
   Wilson's type-1 uses E8 roots = A₁ ∪ Wilson-type-2 (ODD # of minus
   signs).  These are different sets; the 384 A₃-based type-1 vectors do
   NOT satisfy Wilson's membership conditions.

3. Dixon's construction gives a DIFFERENT EMBEDDING of the Leech lattice in
   R^24 from Wilson's.  The two shells share exactly 17,232 vectors.

4. The question of whether Λ₂₄ admits a 24-dimensional integral algebra
   structure remains open.  Dixon's Section 7 acknowledges this explicitly.
   The computational tools in leech_wilson.py and leech_dixon.py provide the
   reference implementation for testing any proposed product.
