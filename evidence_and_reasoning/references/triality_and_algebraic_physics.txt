Topic: Triality and algebraic approaches to physics
=====================================================

This file collects soft references for the broader programme of using
triality and algebraic structures (normed division algebras, Moufang
loops, composition algebras) in mathematical physics.  These references
are cited in the Outlook section of the paper as context for the
proposal that a ternary reformulation of the Leech-lattice order may
be a natural direction.  Entries here are bibliographic pointers for
intuition-building context rather than sources used to establish
technical claims.

### [Hall2019_Moufang]

- **Author**: Jonathan I. Hall
- **Title**: Moufang Loops and Groups with Triality are Essentially the
  Same Thing
- **Published in**: Memoirs of the American Mathematical Society,
  Volume 260, Number 1252 (2019), vi+194 pp.
- **Year**: 2019
- **ISBN**: 978-1-4704-3622-7
- **DOI**: 10.1090/memo/1252
- **Topics**: triality, Moufang loops, groups with triality, octonions
- **Originality note**: Peer-reviewed monograph published in the Memoirs
  of the AMS. No dispute over originality known.
- **Notes**: Establishes a precise correspondence between Moufang loops
  and groups with triality. Seminal modern treatment of triality in the
  loop-theoretic setting, and a natural reference point for any
  ternary-structured reformulation of the construction in this paper.

### [FureyHughes2025_TrioOfTrialities]

- **Authors**: Nichol Furey, Mia J. Hughes
- **Title**: Three generations and a trio of trialities
- **Published in**: Physics Letters B, Volume 865 (2025), Article 139473
- **Year**: 2025
- **arXiv**: 2409.17948 [hep-ph]
- **Topics**: triality, octonions, standard model, division algebras,
  three generations, algebraic physics
- **Originality note**: Peer-reviewed journal article (Phys. Lett. B).
  The arXiv preprint (September 2024) precedes the journal publication
  (2025). No dispute over originality or priority is known at time of
  writing.
- **Notes**: Identifies the Standard Model's
  su(3) ⊕ su(2) ⊕ u(1) internal symmetries inside the triality
  algebras tri(C) ⊕ tri(H) ⊕ tri(O), and applies the resulting action
  to a triality triple (Ψ₊, Ψ₋, V) in C ⊗ H ⊗ O to recover three
  generations of Standard-Model representations (with the third
  generation arising via a Cartan factorization that merges spinor
  representations into a set of scalar bosons including the Higgs
  representation). Cited in this paper's Outlook as the contemporary
  example showing that triality can be isolated as an algebraic
  skeleton into which a variety of particle-family representations
  fit (the authors' own being one specific realization).
- **Status**: Bibliographic entry only; not held in source_documents/.
- **Verification**: arXiv identifier 2409.17948 and journal reference
  Phys. Lett. B 865 (2025) 139473 provided by the human researcher
  in prompt 042.
