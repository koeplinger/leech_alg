Topic: Octonion algebra — background and standard properties
==============================================================

This file collects references for the foundational properties of the octonion
algebra used throughout this project.  These are well-established results in
the public domain.

---

### [Wikipedia-Octonion]

- **Title**: Octonion
- **Source**: Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octonion
- **Topics**: octonions, alternativity, flexibility, associator, Moufang identities,
  composition algebra, division algebra, Fano plane, conjugate, norm
- **Originality note**: Wikipedia is a collaborative encyclopaedia.  The mathematical
  content on this page describes results in the public domain (the octonion algebra
  was discovered by John T. Graves in 1843 and independently by Arthur Cayley in 1845;
  see note on priority below).  Wikipedia itself makes no original mathematical claims.
  No dispute over the content of standard octonion properties is known.
- **Priority note on discovery**: The octonions were first described by John T. Graves
  in a letter to William R. Hamilton dated 26 December 1843.  Arthur Cayley published
  a description independently in 1845, before Graves's work was published, leading to
  the name "Cayley numbers" being used historically.  Hamilton communicated Graves's
  work to the Royal Irish Academy in 1844.  The priority of Graves is acknowledged in
  the modern literature.  References:
    - Baez, J.C., "The Octonions", Bull. Amer. Math. Soc. 39 (2002) 145-205.
      (See Section 1 for the historical account.)
    - Conway, J.H. and Smith, D.A., "On Quaternions and Octonions: Their Geometry,
      Arithmetic and Symmetry", A.K. Peters, 2003.
- **Notes**: Used as the primary reference for standard octonion properties verified
  in python_project/tests/test_octonions.py, including: identity, imaginary squares,
  anticommutativity, alternativity, flexibility, Moufang identities, norm
  multiplicativity, conjugate formula, division algebra property.

### [Schafer1966]

- **Authors**: Richard D. Schafer
- **Title**: Introduction to Nonassociative Algebras
- **Published in**: Academic Press, New York, 1966.  (Dover reprint 1995.)
- **Topics**: alternative algebras, associator, Moufang algebras, Cayley algebras
- **Originality note**: Standard textbook; no dispute known.
- **Notes**: Standard reference for the theorem that, for an alternative algebra,
  the associator is totally antisymmetric (alternating).  Referenced in
  python_project/tests/test_octonions.py (TestAssociatorAntisymmetry) to justify
  that total antisymmetry of the associator on basis elements implies the property
  for all elements by trilinearity.

- **Published in**: Bulletin of the American Mathematical Society, Vol. 39, No. 2

- **URL**: https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0105155

  including the Fano plane description, the connection to triality, and the role

### [Kirmse1924]

- **Authors**: Johannes Kirmse
- **Title**: Über die Darstellbarkeit natürlicher ganzer Zahlen als Summen
  von acht Quadraten und über ein mit diesem Problem zusammenhängendes
  nichtkommutatives und nichtassoziatives Zahlensystem
- **Published in**: Berichte über die Verhandlungen der Königlich
  Sächsischen Gesellschaft der Wissenschaften zu Leipzig, Mathematisch-
  Physische Klasse, Volume 76 (1924), pages 63–82
- **Year**: 1924
- **Topics**: integral octonions, E8 lattice, sums of eight squares
- **Originality note**: Original research paper; Kirmse's sole known
  publication. No dispute over originality known. The paper contains an
  error (claiming the integral octonion lattice is closed under
  multiplication), corrected by Coxeter (1946).
- **Notes**: Kirmse exhibited the E8 lattice as a positive definite
  unimodular integral quadratic lattice of rank 8 inside the real
  octonion algebra. He also published what are presumably the first
  appearance in print of the identities x(x̄y) = n(x)y = (yx̄)x
  (Petersson 2018). See terminology.md for a fuller account of Kirmse's
  contributions alongside the error.

### [Coxeter1946]

- **Authors**: H.S.M. Coxeter
- **Title**: Integral Cayley numbers
- **Published in**: Duke Mathematical Journal, Volume 13, Number 4 (1946),
  pages 561–578
- **Year**: 1946
- **DOI**: 10.1215/S0012-7094-46-01347-6
- **Topics**: integral octonions, maximal orders, index-doubling permutation
- **Originality note**: Published in a peer-reviewed journal. Coxeter
  corrects Kirmse's error and constructs the first explicitly verified
  maximal order of integral octonions via the index-doubling permutation.
  No dispute over originality or priority known.
- **Notes**: Coxeter showed Kirmse's lattice is not closed under
  multiplication for non-associative products. Together with Bruck, he
  introduced the index-doubling permutation (the "Kirmse twist") that
  corrects the construction to a genuine maximal order.

### [ConwaySmith2003]

- **Authors**: John H. Conway, Derek A. Smith
- **Title**: On Quaternions and Octonions: Their Geometry, Arithmetic, and
  Symmetry
- **Published in**: A.K. Peters, Natick, Massachusetts, 2003
- **Year**: 2003
- **ISBN**: 1-56881-134-9
- **Topics**: quaternions, octonions, integral octonions, maximal orders,
  E8 lattice, Leech lattice
- **Originality note**: Published monograph. Standard reference for
  integral octonion arithmetic. No dispute known.
- **Notes**: Chapter 9 covers maximal orders of integral octonions and the
  index-doubling permutation. Used throughout this project as the primary

### [ConwaySloane1999]

- **Authors**: John H. Conway, Neil J.A. Sloane
- **Title**: Sphere Packings, Lattices and Groups
- **Published in**: Springer-Verlag, New York, 3rd edition, 1999
  (Grundlehren der mathematischen Wissenschaften, Volume 290)
- **Year**: 1999
- **ISBN**: 0-387-98585-9
- **Topics**: Leech lattice, sphere packings, lattices, error-correcting
  codes, E8 lattice, Niemeier lattices
- **Originality note**: Standard reference monograph. No dispute known.
- **Notes**: Chapter 4 covers the Leech lattice. Chapter 12 contains
  Conway's uniqueness theorem for the Leech lattice (the unique even
  unimodular lattice of rank 24 with no vectors of squared norm 2).
  Cited in this project for the Leech lattice's basic properties and for
  the fact that Min(Λ) generates Λ over Z.

### [Petersson2018]

- **Authors**: Holger P. Petersson
- **Title**: Integral octonions
- **Published in**: Lecture at the Málaga Workshop on Non-Associative
  Algebras, September 6, 2018
- **Year**: 2018
- **URL**: https://www.fernuni-hagen.de/mi/fakultaet/emeriti/docs/petersson/ass.-rem.-int.-oct.pdf
- **Topics**: integral octonions, Kirmse, Coxeter, Dickson, maximal orders,
  historical account
- **Originality note**: Lecture notes; not formally peer-reviewed. Provides
  the most detailed modern historical account of the development of
  integral octonion theory from Kirmse (1924) through Coxeter (1946).
- **Notes**: Petersson treats Kirmse with respect, calling his work "the
  bold attempt of a young mathematician." Documents Dickson's (1923)
  prior construction of integral octonions isomorphic to Coxeter's.
  Source for the claim that Kirmse's identities first appeared in print
  in 1924.

### [Baez2014_IntegralOctonions]

- **Authors**: John C. Baez (with contributions by Greg Egan and others)
- **Title**: Integral Octonions, Parts 1–12
- **Published in**: The n-Category Café (group blog), 2014
- **Year**: 2014
- **URL**: https://golem.ph.utexas.edu/category/2014/11/integral_octonions_part_9.html
  (Part 9); https://math.ucr.edu/home/baez/octonions/integers/index.html
  (series index)
- **Topics**: integral octonions, Leech lattice, exceptional Jordan
  algebra, Jordan ring, multiplicative closure
- **Originality note**: Blog series; not peer-reviewed. Contains original
  computations by Baez and Egan, and open questions.
- **Notes**: Parts 9–10 explore whether the Leech lattice can be made into
  a ring via the exceptional Jordan algebra 𝔥₃(O). Key findings:
  (1) The lattice Z³ ⊕ L_L ⊂ 𝔥₃(O) is NOT closed under the standard
  Jordan product x ∘ y = xy + yx, but IS closed under the doubled
  product 2(xy + yx). (2) Baez poses the open question of finding an
  "exceptional Leech–Jordan ring." (3) Egan finds 17,280 sublattices
  yielding formally real Jordan rings under the doubled product.
  The human researcher of this project (Koeplinger) participated in the
  discussion, reporting that Jordan products did not close on the Leech
  inner shell. This is the closest prior art to the present finding, but
  uses a fundamentally different algebraic framework (commutative Jordan
  product, 27-dimensional) rather than the non-commutative
  octonion-derived product in 24 dimensions used here.
