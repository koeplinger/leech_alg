Prompt 019 — Ideas for additional tests to rule out the triple-octonion algebra
================================================================================
Date: 2026-04-09

User prompt (verbatim):
-----------------------
Great. With these updates, and the existing two trials for the product made from
three octonions, is there an additional test that you can think of to reasonable
rule out this algebra from being an order that closes on the Leech? We already
have enough evidence that it doesn't, here I'm looking for thoroughness of our
methodology. I give one example of a test: trivial reordering of coefficient
mappings. Rather than prescribing the coefficient mappings from the octonion
algebras into the Leech lattice, we could permit any permutation thereof. There
are a lot of permutations, of course, and certainly not all need to be checked.
This is an idea for another test. There may be other ideas. In a first step,
give me some ideas. I will then see whether we pick on of these for the algebra
of the first two tests, or whether we make the move to test another algebra.

Actions taken:
--------------
Presented six ideas for additional tests, ordered by relevance to the known
failure mode (condition 3, type3×type3):

1. Conjugation variants on cross-block terms (ā·b, a·b̄, ā·b̄).
   4³ = 64 combinations, fewer after Z₃ symmetry. Directly targets the
   Ls/Ls̄ structure that condition 3 governs.

2. Sign flips on cross-block terms (±1 per cross-block pair).
   2³ = 8 patterns (4 up to Z₃ symmetry). Simplest discrete variant.

3. Permutation of basis indices within blocks (user's suggestion).
   Only permutations preserving the E8 lattice L are relevant. The meaningful
   space is Aut(L) modulo Aut(O) ∩ Aut(L). Could apply different permutations
   to different blocks.

4. Different block-to-block routing.
   Alternative to Oα × Oβ → Oγ: cross products could stay in the left or
   right factor's block. Breaks Z₃ symmetry. Only 2–3 routing rules.

5. Combined sweep of ideas 1+2+4.
   Full discrete parameter space is small (~500 combinations). Recommended
   as the highest-value single test for this algebra.

6. Non-diagonal basis change within blocks (GL(8) per block).
   Generalises trial 002's diagonal scaling. Continuous, high-dimensional
   (64 params/block). Could restrict to Aut(L) to keep it finite.

Recommendation: ideas 1+2+4 as a single exhaustive sweep. If the entire
discrete family fails, that is a much stronger negative result than
trials 001–002 alone.
