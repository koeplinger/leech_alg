--- FINDING: WILSON'S E8 CONSTRUCTION ---

Wilson [Wilson2009] Section 2 defines the E8 lattice L as the Z-span of 240 octonions:

  Type-1 (112 roots):  ±i_t ± i_u  for distinct t, u in PL(7)
  Type-2 (128 roots):  (1/2)(±1 ± i_0 ± ... ± i_6)  with an ODD number of minus signs.

Under the index map  Wilson i_inf <-> e_0,  Wilson i_k <-> e_{k+1}  (established in
key claim 001), this becomes:

  Type-1 (112 roots):  ±e_a ± e_b  for distinct a, b in {0,...,7}
  Type-2 (128 roots):  (1/2)(±e_0 ± e_1 ± ... ± e_7)  with an ODD number of minus signs.

Wilson's special element s = (1/2)(-1 + i_0 + ... + i_6) becomes:
  s = (1/2)(-e_0 + e_1 + ... + e_7)
which is a type-2 root (1 minus sign = odd).

Wilson additionally proves:
  N(s) = 2  (s is a minimal vector of L)
  Ls = 2B   (B = Coxeter-Dickson ring, another E8 copy)
  2L ⊂ Ls ⊂ L

Lattice membership criterion (D8+ = E8 characterisation):
  L = { x in R^8 : all x_i in Z,     sum(x_i) even }
    u { x in R^8 : all x_i in Z+1/2, sum(x_i) odd  }

Self-duality / unimodularity:
  A Z-basis of L that covers BOTH cosets of D8 is required to see det = 1.
  The basis {WILSON_S, e_0+e_1, e_0-e_1, e_0+e_2, e_0+e_3, e_0+e_4, e_0+e_5, e_0+e_6}
  has det(B) = 1 (verified analytically by cofactor expansion along the e_7 column).
  Bases consisting only of type-1 roots span the D8 sublattice of index 2 (det = 4).
