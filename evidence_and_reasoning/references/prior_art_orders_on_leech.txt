Topic: Prior art — multiplicative structures / orders on the Leech lattice
=============================================================================

Literature search conducted: 2026-04-11
Purpose: Identify any prior work establishing or ruling out an order (or
ring, algebra, multiplicatively closed structure) on the Leech lattice,
before publishing our finding.

---

## Summary of search

**No prior work was found establishing an order on the Leech lattice
under an octonion-derived bilinear product.**

The specific construction in this project — three copies of a
transposition-twisted octonion algebra with Z₃ cross-block routing —
does not appear in the literature.

The closest related work falls into three categories:

1. **Wilson (2009)**: Characterises Λ as a sublattice of L³ using
   octonionic conditions, but does not define or test a multiplicative
   product on Λ.

2. **Dixon (1995, 2010)**: Proposes octonionic descriptions of Λ using
   variants of the octonion product (XY-product in the 2010 formal
   preprint, earlier versions going back to the 1995 arXiv preprints),
   but does not establish closure of Λ under any such product.

3. **Baez / Egan (2014)**: Explores whether the Leech lattice can be
   made into a ring via the exceptional Jordan algebra (27-dimensional,
   commutative).  Finds that an integer sublattice of 𝔥₃(O) containing
   a Leech embedding is closed under the doubled Jordan product
   2(xy + yx), but this involves a factor-of-2 scaling and the Jordan
   (commutative) product, not an octonion-derived (non-commutative,
   non-associative) product.  The question of closure under the
   undoubled Jordan product is left open, with a conjectured negative
   answer.  The human researcher of this project (Koeplinger)
   participated in that discussion.

## Detailed findings

### [Wilson2009] — Octonions and the Leech lattice

Wilson constructs Λ as {(x,y,z) ∈ L³ : conditions 1–3}, gives the
explicit decomposition 196,560 = 3 × 240 × (1 + 16 + 16²) of the
minimal shell into three families, and proves Λ is even and self-dual
by elementary arguments using L and the sublattices Ls, Ls̄.  The
paper does not describe the Conway group; it announces at the end
that a follow-on paper ("Conway's group and octonions", listed as
reference [20] of Wilson 2009 and submitted at the time) will
generate Aut(Λ) using 3×3 octonionic matrices.  See the [WilsonConway]
entry below for that follow-on.  Wilson 2009 does not define or test
a bilinear product on Λ; it uses the octonionic structure for the
sublattice characterisation only.

### [Dixon1995] — Octonions: invariant Leech lattice exposed

Dixon's 1995 arXiv preprint (hep-th/9506080, 12 June 1995; preceded by
the closely related hep-th/9504040, 7 April 1995, "Octonions:
invariant representation of the Leech lattice") proposes an early
octonionic description of Λ.  A conjectured formula for one of the
three minimal-vector families needed a small correction: Wilson
supplied this correction in a footnote-style remark at the end of §5
of Wilson 2009, while independently deriving the sublattice
characterisation above.  The Dixon–Wilson situation is structurally
analogous to the Kirmse–Coxeter one: an early, structurally correct
proposal refined by a later author's short correction.  Dixon 1995
does not test or claim closure of Λ under any bilinear product.

### [Dixon2010] — Integral Octonions, Octonion XY-Product, and the Leech Lattice

Dixon's 2010 preprint (arXiv:1011.2541) constructs Λ using the
octonion XY-product

    A ∘_{XY} B  =  (A X)(Y† B),

where X, Y ∈ O are chosen so that Y X† is a unit octonion (the
identity of the new multiplication).  The special case Y† = X⁻¹ gives
the X-product A ∘_X B = (A X)(X⁻¹ B).  Dixon uses the X-product with
X = ℓ₀ = ½(1 + e₁ + … + e₇) to obtain E₈, and general XY-products for
higher laminated lattices including Λ.  Each XY-product is an
alternative octonion multiplication on R⁸ (isomorphic to the
standard one).  Dixon does not test or claim closure of Λ under the
XY-product or any other bilinear product.

### [Baez2014] — Integral Octonions, parts 9–10 (n-Category Café)

John Baez's blog series (November–December 2014) explores the
exceptional Jordan algebra 𝔥₃(O) as a framework for the Leech lattice.
Parts 9–10 contain the Leech-Jordan material.  Baez considers the
27-dimensional lattice ℤ³ ⊕ L_L ⊂ 𝔥₃(O) with integers on the
diagonal and a Leech sublattice L_L ≅ Λ off-diagonal.  Key findings:

- The lattice ℤ³ ⊕ L_L ⊂ 𝔥₃(O) is NOT closed under the Jordan product
  x ∘ y = xy + yx.
- It IS closed under the doubled Jordan product 2(xy + yx).
- Egan computationally enumerated at least 17,280 distinct off-diagonal
  Leech embeddings L_L ⊂ O³ for which the associated 27-dimensional
  lattice is closed under the doubled product.  (The total number of
  Leech embeddings he found in O³ is much larger — at least
  244,035,421 — but only the 17,280 are compatible with Jordan
  closure.)
- Baez left as an open question whether any such lattice is closed
  under the undoubled Jordan product xy + yx, which would remove the
  factor-of-2 discrepancy, and conjectured that the answer is
  negative ("I suspect the answer is no!").
- Baez expresses dissatisfaction with the factor of 2: "the factor of 2
  in condition c) really annoys me."
- **The human researcher (Koeplinger) is quoted in the discussion**:
  "I ran several kinds of (exceptional) Jordan products through my little
  program...but essentially didn't find anything that would close on the
  Leech inner shell."

This is the closest prior art to our finding, but uses a fundamentally
different algebraic framework (commutative Jordan product in 27
dimensions) rather than a non-commutative, non-associative
octonion-derived product in 24 dimensions.

### [WilsonConway] — Conway's group and octonions

Wilson's follow-on to Wilson 2009 describes the double cover of
Conway's group Co₀ using right multiplications by 3×3 octonionic
matrices.  This uses the octonion product to describe symmetries, not
to define a closed algebra on Λ.
