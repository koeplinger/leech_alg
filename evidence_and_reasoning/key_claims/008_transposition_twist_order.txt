Title: The transposition-twisted triple octonion product is an order on the Leech lattice Λ
Status: ESTABLISHED (symbolic proof + independent computational verification)
Date: 2026-04-12

--- RELATION TO CLAIM 007 ---

Claim 007 (2026-04-09) rules out the *untwisted* triple-octonion algebra,
together with all natural discrete and continuous modifications tested
at that time, as an order on Λ.  Claim 008 is not a revision of 007:
claim 007 remains true of the family it covers.  Claim 008 records a
positive finding obtained by leaving that family — specifically, by
twisting the single octonion multiplication used in all three blocks
via a transposition of imaginary basis elements, a construction not
included in the discrete variants exhausted under claim 007.

Per Manifesto Section 11 (Forward-evolving corrections), this file is
a new entry recording a subsequent finding; it does not alter claim 007.

--- STATEMENT ---

Let σ be a transposition of two imaginary basis elements of the
octonion algebra O (e.g. σ = (e₁ e₂)).  Define a new bilinear
multiplication on R⁸ by

    x ·_σ y := σ( σ(x) · σ(y) ),

where · is the standard octonion product.  Equivalently, σ : (O, ·)
→ (O, ·_σ) is an isomorphism of algebras, but σ does not preserve the
Wilson E8 lattice L.

Define the transposition-twisted triple octonion product on
R²⁴ = O₁ ⊕ O₂ ⊕ O₃ by using ·_σ in all three blocks with Z₃-symmetric
cross-block routing (same-block Oα × Oα → Oα; cross-block Oα × Oβ → Oγ
for {α, β, γ} = {1, 2, 3}).

Then the Leech lattice Λ (in Wilson's E8-triple realisation) is closed
under this product.  Consequently (Λ, +, ·_σ-triple) is an order in
the real non-associative R-algebra (R²⁴, +, ·_σ-triple).

--- PROOF ---

The symbolic proof is written out in full in paper/main.tex, Section 4.
It reduces closure on Min(Λ) (hence, by bilinearity, on Λ) to five
lemmas:

  Lemma 4.1:  σ(L) = L          (coordinate-permutation invariance of D₈⁺)
  Lemma 4.2:  L · L ⊆ L         (L is a maximal order; Coxeter 1946)
  Lemma 4.3:  L · σ(Ls̄) ⊆ σ(Ls̄)
              (σ(Ls̄) is a left L-module under the standard product)
  Lemma 4.4:  σ(Ls) · σ(Ls) ⊆ σ(Ls)
              (σ(Ls) is closed under the standard product)
  Lemma 4.5:  σ(Ls) ≠ Ls
              (so the twist is doing nontrivial work)

The core structural point: v ·_σ w, expanded via the bilinearity
decomposition, reduces each of Wilson's three sublattice conditions to
a statement about standard octonion products of the σ-images of the
input blocks.  Wilson's condition (3) becomes σ(Ls) · σ(Ls) ⊆ σ(Ls),
which holds (Lemma 4.4); the analogous statement for Ls itself is
*false* for the untwisted product — this is precisely the condition-3
failure recorded in claim 007.

Lemmas 4.3 and 4.4 are *finite* verifications: each asserts closure of
a product of 8 Z-basis vectors against 8 Z-basis vectors, i.e. 64
products per lemma.  These are discharged by exact rational arithmetic.

--- WHAT DOES AND DOES NOT FOLLOW ---

Follows:
 - (Λ, +, ·_σ-triple) is an order in the real R-algebra (R²⁴, +, ·_σ-triple).
 - The obstruction identified in claim 007 (failure of Wilson condition
   (3) for type3 × type3) is specific to the *untwisted* standard
   product in that block structure; a single transposition twist
   removes it.

Does NOT follow:
 - Any claim about associativity, alternativity, flexibility, power-
   associativity, unitality, or norm multiplicativity.  See Section 5
   of the paper and the discussion in CURRENT_STATE.md: the order is
   non-unital, non-commutative, and not norm-multiplicative.
 - Any claim that this order is maximal, or a claim about its
   automorphism group, its relationship to Co₀, or uniqueness up to
   isomorphism.  These are open questions.
