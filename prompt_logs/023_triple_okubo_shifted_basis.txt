Actions taken:
--------------
1. Investigated the user's request: construct three Okubo algebras from the
   same Fano line by cyclically rotating which element serves as mediator.
   Discovered that for EVERY Fano line, only 1 or 2 of the 3 cyclic mediators
   yield valid order-3 automorphisms — no line admits all three.  The user's
   original "shifted mediator" construction is mathematically impossible.

2. Designed the closest valid alternative: a Z₃ orbit of Petersson isotopes
   {τ⁰, τ¹, τ²} where τ is the standard order-3 octonion automorphism that
   fixes {e₀, e₁, e₃, e₇} and rotates (e₂, e₅), (e₄, e₆) by 2π/3.
   This gives three distinct symmetric composition algebras:
   - Block 0: para-octonion (x * y = x̄ · ȳ)
   - Block 1: Okubo_τ (x * y = τ(x̄) · τ²(ȳ))
   - Block 2: Okubo_τ² (x * y = τ²(x̄) · τ(ȳ))

3. Implemented trial_005_triple_okubo.py:
   - Base algebra test on all type combinations (1,600 pairs)
   - Full discrete variant sweep (1,536 variants: 4³ conjugation × 2³ sign × 3 routing)
   - Result: 6.9% closure; 91.5% of failures leave E8 lattice entirely
   - Root cause: Petersson construction introduces irrational √3 structure constants

4. Implemented trial_006_triple_okubo_automorphisms.py:
   - E8 automorphism basis changes (300 single-block, 300 three-block)
   - Result: 0% baseline, 3.8% best; √3 cannot be absorbed by E8 automorphisms

5. Recorded results in evidence_and_reasoning/trial_005_results.md and
   trial_006_results.md.

Key finding: The Petersson construction with any non-trivial order-3
automorphism τ introduces cos(2π/3) = −1/2 and sin(2π/3) = √3/2 into the
structure constants.  Products of E8 lattice vectors then acquire irrational
√3 components that cannot lie in the E8 lattice.  This is a more fundamental
obstruction than the triple-octonion failure (which preserved E8 membership
but failed on Wilson condition 3).
