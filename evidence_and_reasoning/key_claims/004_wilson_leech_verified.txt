Title: Wilson's octonionic construction yields the 196,560 minimal vectors of the Leech lattice
Status: ESTABLISHED
Date: 2026-04-07

--- STATEMENT ---

Wilson [Wilson2009 Section 3] defines the Leech lattice as:

    Λ = { (x, y, z) ∈ L³ :
          (1)  x, y, z ∈ L
          (2)  x+y, x+z, y+z ∈ Ls̄
          (3)  x+y+z ∈ Ls }

where L is the E8 lattice, s = ½(−e₀+e₁+...+e₇) (WILSON_S),
s̄ = conjugate of s, and:

    Ls̄ = {λ × s̄ : λ ∈ L}   (index-16 sublattice of L, = 2B)
    Ls  = {λ × s  : λ ∈ L}   (index-16 sublattice of L)

The 196,560 minimal vectors of Λ (standard R^24 norm-squared = 8) fall into
three families:

    Type 1:  (2λ, 0, 0) + 2 cyclic perms          — 3 × 240      =    720
    Type 2:  (λs̄, (λs̄)j, 0) + 2 cyclic perms     — 3 × 240 × 16 = 11,520
    Type 3:  ((λs)j, λk, (λj)k) + 2 cyclic perms  — 3 × 240 × 16 × 16 = 184,320

    Total: 196,560 = 3 × 240 × (1 + 16 + 256)

where λ runs over the 240 E8 roots and j, k ∈ J = {±e_t : t = 0,...,7}.

CRITICAL NOTE ON TYPE-3 FORMULA:
Type-2 uses s̄ in BOTH components.
Type-3 uses s (not s̄) in the FIRST component.
Using s̄ in type-3 produces 184,320 norm-8 triples that do NOT satisfy
Wilson's membership conditions.  This distinction is verified computationally
and is also explicitly noted in Wilson's paper:
  "Dixon's formula is equivalent to (λs, ±λk, ±(λj)k), that is, omitting the
   (necessary) multiplication by j in the first coordinate."
  — [Wilson2009 p. 2189, footnote on Dixon's construction]

All three families are verified to satisfy conditions (1)–(3) above.

--- SUBLATTICE STRUCTURES ---

Ls̄ = 2B:
  Z-basis: images of an L Z-basis under right-mult by s̄.
  Gram det: 256 (index [L : Ls̄] = 16, so [L : Ls̄]² × det(Gram_L) = 256).
  Minimum norm: N(λ) × N(s̄) = 2 × 2 = 4 (no norm-2 elements, so no E8 roots).

Ls:
  Z-basis: images of an L Z-basis under right-mult by s.
  Gram det: 256 (index [L : Ls] = 16, same argument).
  Minimum norm: 4.

Key relations:
  Ls̄ ∩ Ls = 2L  (all 2×root are in both; minimum norm = 8)
  Ls̄ + Ls = L   (combined generators span L; verified by rank check)
  2L ⊆ Ls̄ and 2L ⊆ Ls

--- NORM AND COUNT VERIFICATION ---

Type-1: N_std(2λ, 0, 0) = 4 N(λ) = 4 × 2 = 8. Count: 3 × 240 = 720. ✓
Type-2: N_std(λs̄, (λs̄)j, 0) = N(λ)N(s̄) + N(λ)N(s̄)N(j) = 4 + 4 = 8.
        Count: 3 × 240 × 16 = 11,520. ✓
Type-3: N_std((λs)j, λk, (λj)k) = N(λ)N(s)N(j) + N(λ)N(k) + N(λ)N(j)N(k)
                                  = 4 + 2 + 2 = 8.
        Count: 3 × 240 × 16 × 16 = 184,320. ✓
Total: 720 + 11,520 + 184,320 = 196,560. ✓

--- LATTICE PROPERTIES ---

Rank 24: the 196,560 minimal vectors span R^24.

Even lattice: the full 720×720 Gram matrix of type-1 vectors has all entries
in 2Z (Wilson inner product = ½ × standard dot product is always an integer).

Self-dual (det_Wilson = 1): proved analytically in [Wilson2009 Section 4].
Under the standard R^24 Euclidean inner product, det(Gram_Λ) = 2^24 (because
Wilson's norm is ½ of the standard norm, scaling the Gram det by 2^24).

No norm-2 vectors (proven analytically):
  If (x, y, z) ∈ Λ has N_std < 8, at least one coordinate is 0.
  If z = 0: conditions (2) and (3) give x+y ∈ Ls̄ and x+y ∈ Ls, so
    x+y ∈ Ls̄ ∩ Ls = 2L.  Also x ∈ Ls̄ and y ∈ Ls̄.
  If y = z = 0: condition (3) gives x ∈ Ls, and condition (2) gives x ∈ Ls̄,
    so x ∈ 2L, hence N(x) ≥ 8.  Contradiction.
  Therefore min norm = 8 (minimum is achieved by the 196,560 minimal vectors).

These four properties uniquely characterise the Leech lattice by Conway's
theorem [Conway–Sloane, Chapter 12].

--- THE LEECH LATTICE AS AN INTEGRAL OCTONION LATTICE ---

Λ is a rank-24 Z-module (lattice) embedded inside L³, where
L = E8 = the integral octonions (the Coxeter–Dickson order).  Because L
carries the octonion product, L³ inherits candidate algebraic structure, and
the membership conditions (1)–(3) characterise exactly which elements of L³
belong to Λ.  The question of whether an octonion-based multiplication rule
on R^24 restricts to a product that closes on Λ — making Λ a 24-dimensional
integral algebra — is the central research question of this project.  Dixon
[Dixon2010] has examined this question; the tools established here (the
membership test is_in_leech and the minimal-vector shell) provide the
reference for evaluating any such proposal.
