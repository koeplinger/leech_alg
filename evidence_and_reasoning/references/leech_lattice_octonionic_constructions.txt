Topic: Octonionic constructions of the Leech lattice
=========================================================

This file collects references for works that construct, or contribute to constructing,
the Leech lattice using octonions, the E8 lattice, and related structures.

---

### [Wilson2009]

- **Authors**: Robert A. Wilson
- **Title**: Octonions and the Leech lattice
- **Published in**: Journal of Algebra, Volume 322, Issue 7, 2009, pages 2186–2190
- **Year**: 2009
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jalgebra.2009.03.021
- **Article history**: Received 18 December 2008; available online 27 March 2009;
  communicated by Gernot Stroth
- **Affiliation**: School of Mathematical Sciences, Queen Mary, University of London,
  Mile End Road, London E1 4NS, United Kingdom
- **Source**: Not redistributed locally (Elsevier publisher copyright).
  Canonical DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jalgebra.2009.03.021
- **Topics**: Leech lattice, octonions, E8 lattice, octonionic Leech lattice,
  Coxeter–Dickson non-associative ring, minimal vectors, Conway group
- **Originality note**: Published in a peer-reviewed journal (Journal of Algebra,
  Elsevier). Wilson claims this construction is "new" and provides "the first real
  explanation" of why the minimal vector count 196560 factors as
  3 × 240 × (1 + 16 + 16 × 16). The paper explicitly situates itself relative to
  prior constructions (referencing 23 other constructions cited in [3] and 4
  constructions in [16] of Wilson's bibliography). No dispute over priority or
  originality is known to this project at time of writing. If a dispute is
  discovered later it must be added here.
- **Notes**: The construction defines the octonionic Leech lattice Λ as the set of
  triples (x, y, z) of octonions from the E8 lattice L (specifically the
  Coxeter–Dickson ring A), subject to three closure conditions. This 3-coordinate
  structure — three copies of an octonionic E8 representation — is directly
  relevant to the hypothesis under investigation in this project.

### [Dixon2010]

- **Authors**: Geoffrey Dixon
- **Title**: Integral Octonions, Octonion XY-Product, and the Leech Lattice
- **Published in**: UNPUBLISHED PREPRINT. No journal imprint appears in the document.
  Author affiliation listed as www.7stones.com (Dixon's personal website).
- **Year**: 2010 (document dated November 21, 2010)
- **URL**: Dixon's site https://www.7stones.com (no explicit license).
- **Source**: Not redistributed locally (no explicit license from author).
- **Topics**: Leech lattice, integral octonions, XY-product, X-product, E8 lattice,
  laminated lattices in dimensions 4, 8, 16, 24
- **Originality note**: This is an unpublished preprint and has not undergone formal
  peer review. The XY-product construction referenced as [1] in Dixon's paper is
  attributed to Dixon's earlier work; no dispute over that is known to this project.
  Because the document has not been peer-reviewed, independent verification of its
  claims is required before they are relied upon in this project. No plagiarism
  dispute is known at time of writing.
- **Notes**: Dixon constructs representations of the E8 lattice (Λ8) using the
  X-product variant of octonion multiplication, and extends this to reach the
  24-dimensional Leech lattice via a construction involving three copies. The
  XY-product (A ∘_XY B := (AX)(Y†B)) is central to the paper and differs from
  standard octonion multiplication. This approach is a second independent line of
  evidence for the three-copies-of-E8 structure that the hypothesis aims to
  reformulate using the Okubo algebra.
