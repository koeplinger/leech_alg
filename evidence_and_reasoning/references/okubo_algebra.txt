Topic: Okubo algebra — constructions, properties, triality, and applications
=====================================================================================

This file collects references for the Okubo (pseudo-octonion) algebra and
related structures: symmetric composition algebras, triality, automorphisms
and derivations of composition algebras, and applications.

---

### [SmithVojtechovsky2022]

- **Authors**: Jonathan D. H. Smith, Petr Vojtěchovský
- **Title**: Okubo quasigroups
- **Published in**: Documenta Mathematica, Volume 27 (2022), pages 535–580
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.4171/DM/877
- **Source file**: SmithVojtechovsky_Okubo_2022_DocMath.pdf
- **Topics**: okubo_algebra, background
- **Originality note**: Published in a peer-reviewed journal (Documenta
  Mathematica, EMS Press). No dispute over originality or priority is known
  at time of writing.
- **Notes**: Studies quasigroup structures obtained by replacing split
  octonion multiplication with multiplication from para-octonion and Okubo
  algebras. Covers multiplication groups, power structure, generating sets,
  subquasigroups, and automorphisms of Okubo quasigroups. Provides an
  algebraic-combinatorial perspective on the Okubo product that complements
  the purely algebraic treatments in other references.

### [MarraniCorradettiZucconi2025]

- **Authors**: Alessio Marrani, Daniele Corradetti, Francesco Zucconi
- **Title**: Physics with non-unital algebras? An invitation to the Okubo algebra
- **Published in**: Journal of Physics A: Mathematical and Theoretical,
  Volume 58 (2025), Article 075202
- **Year**: 2025 (arXiv preprint 2023)
- **DOI**: 10.1088/1751-8121/adafef
- **arXiv**: 2309.17435 [hep-th]
- **Source**: Externally hosted on arXiv (license: nonexclusive-distrib/1.0;
  third-party redistribution not granted). URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.17435
- **Topics**: okubo_algebra, octonions
- **Originality note**: Published in a peer-reviewed journal (J. Phys. A,
  IOP Publishing). The arXiv preprint (v1: September 2023) predates the
  journal publication (2025). No dispute over originality or priority is
  known at time of writing.
- **Notes**: Comprehensive treatment of the Okubo algebra from a physics
  perspective. Covers: the direct matrix construction on traceless 3×3
  Hermitian matrices with product x*y = μ·xy + μ̄·yx − (1/3)Tr(xy); the
  Petersson construction from octonions via an order-3 automorphism τ; the
  norm n(x) = (1/6)Tr(x²); the idempotent e satisfying e*e = e; recovery
  of the octonionic product via x·y = (e*x)*(y*e); Aut(Okubo) = SU(3);
  and conjectured connections to QCD (gluons as Okubo algebra elements).

### [Elduque2000_Triality]

- **Authors**: Alberto Elduque
- **Title**: On triality and automorphisms and derivations of composition algebras
- **Published in**: Linear Algebra and its Applications, Volume 314,
  Issues 1–3 (2000), pages 49–74
- **Year**: 2000
- **DOI**: 10.1016/S0024-3795(00)00105-1
- **Source**: Not redistributed locally (Elsevier publisher copyright).
  Canonical DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0024-3795(00)00105-1
- **Topics**: okubo_algebra, octonions, background
- **Originality note**: Published in a peer-reviewed journal (Linear Algebra
  and its Applications, Elsevier). No dispute over originality or priority
  is known at time of writing.
- **Notes**: Develops the Principle of Local Triality for derivations and
  orthogonal Lie algebras of symmetric composition algebras. Computes
  automorphism groups and derivation algebras: for Okubo algebras,
  Aut = SU(3) and Der = su(3) (the 8-dimensional Lie algebra of type A₂).
  Establishes that symmetric composition algebras (para-Hurwitz and Okubo)
  satisfy (x*y)*x = x*(y*x) = n(x)y. Central reference for the triality
  framework connecting octonions, para-octonions, and Okubo algebras.
