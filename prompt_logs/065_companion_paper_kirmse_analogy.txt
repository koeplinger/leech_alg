--START FEEDBACK--
Your octonionic multiplication via the \sigma twist is an isomorphic
copy of the original multiplication in O. Your cross product is also
an isomorphic copy via \sigma since the original products are
replaced by the \sigma twisted products. So, it seems to me, the
entire setup has just been renamed. This should preserve all
algebraic properties, including closure, so if your new lattice is
closed under *, the old one should be closed as well. What am I
missing? Maybe you are not moving the actual lattice construction by
\sigma? But if that's the case, then you can keep the original
multiplication in O and the original cross product and you can just
shift the lattice construction by the inverse of \sigma (in this case
\sigma^{-1}=\sigma, of course) and find a copy of the Leech lattice
that is closed under *.
--END FEEDBACK--
