--- EVIDENCE FOR CLAIM 1a (WILSON — FULLY ESTABLISHED) ---

Wilson's Λ is verified to satisfy all four defining properties of the Leech
lattice [Conway–Sloane Chapter 12]:

  (i)   Even lattice: type-1 Gram matrix verified to have all entries in 2Z
        (test_even_lattice_type1_gram in test_leech_wilson.py). ✓
  (ii)  Self-dual (det_Wilson = 1): analytic proof in [Wilson2009 Section 4].
        Not reproduced computationally but referenced. ✓
  (iii) Rank 24: verified (test_rank_24 in test_leech_wilson.py). ✓
  (iv)  No norm-2 vectors: analytic proof in [Wilson2009 Section 4]; also
        computationally confirmed by test_single_root_not_in_leech (all 240
        E8 roots (norm 2) fail membership). ✓

  All 196,560 minimal vectors verified to satisfy membership conditions
  (is_in_leech tests for all three families). ✓

  By Conway's uniqueness theorem, these four properties characterise the
  Leech lattice uniquely (up to isometry).

--- EVIDENCE FOR CLAIM 1b (DIXON — STRUCTURAL VERIFICATION) ---

Dixon's construction produces 196,560 candidate minimal vectors satisfying:

  (i)   All 196,560 distinct (no duplicates). ✓
  (ii)  All have N_std = 8. ✓
  (iii) Span R^24 (rank 24). ✓
  (iv)  No norm-2 vectors (all norms are 8 by direct calculation). ✓

  These are necessary but NOT sufficient for the full Leech lattice
  characterization.  Two properties are NOT independently verified:

  (v)   Even lattice property: NOT TESTED for Dixon's shell.
        Gap: the type-1 even-lattice test (Gram entries in 2Z) has been
        performed only for Wilson's type-1.  A corresponding test for Dixon's
        type-1 should be added for full rigour.

  (vi)  Self-duality: NOT PROVEN for Dixon's lattice.
        Gap: no analytic proof of det = 1 has been produced or reproduced
        for the lattice generated by Dixon's 196,560 vectors.

  These properties are trusted from [Dixon2010] but represent an honest gap
  in the independent computational verification.

--- EVIDENCE FOR THE "DIFFERENT EMBEDDINGS" CONCLUSION ---

Computationally established (test_full_shells_differ):

  Shared vectors:      17,232  (type-1 A₁: 336; type-2: 6,144; type-3: 10,752)
  Dixon-only vectors:  179,328
  Wilson-only vectors: 179,328

  Root cause: Dixon uses A^odd = A₁ ∪ A₃ (EVEN # of minus signs); Wilson uses
  E8 roots = A₁ ∪ type-2 (ODD # of minus signs).  The half-integer families
  A₃ and Wilson-type-2 are complementary (disjoint, together covering all 256
  sign patterns).

  Both shells have the same count (196,560), same norm (8), and the same rank
  (24).  They are different representations of the Leech lattice minimal shell
  in the same ambient space R^24 = L³.

--- EVIDENCE FOR CLAIM 2 (INTEGRAL ALGEBRA — OPEN) ---

What both constructions establish:
  Λ₂₄ is a rank-24 Z-module (free module of rank 24 over the integers)
  embedded inside L³, where L = E8 = integral octonions.  Because L carries
  the octonion product, the question "does some octonion-derived product close
  on Λ?" is well-posed and natural.

What neither construction establishes:
  No product on Λ₂₄ is defined by either Wilson or Dixon.

  Wilson [Wilson2009]: constructs Λ as a SET via membership conditions.
  No multiplication is proposed.  Key claim 004: "Wilson does NOT define a
  'multiplication that closes on the Leech lattice.'"

  Dixon [Dixon2010]: defines XY-products on E8 (8-dimensional), and asks in
  Section 7 whether Λ₂₄ can be given its own algebraic structure.  His answer:
  "No idea yet."

  This is the central unsolved question of the research program (stated in
  leech_wilson.py docstring: "The central research question of this project:
  whether a proposed octonion-based multiplication rule on R^24 restricts to
  a product on Λ that closes").
